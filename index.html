Emma Schwartzman, Ph.D. candidate in radio astrophysics.

I am a Ph.D. candidate at George Mason University, working with scientists at the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory.

Radio Astrophysics

Dual AGN, Galaxy cluster mergers, Dwarf galaxies, and more!

My research is spread across a variety of topics and systems, but the overarching theme is radio astrophysics. I am very passionate about radio astrophysics, being an astronomer, and exploring our universe through the radio lens. My research interests include radio galaxies, active galactic nuclei, galaxy clusters, dark matter, dwarf galaxies, intermediate-mass black holes, and much more!
