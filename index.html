Radio Astrophysics

Dual AGN, galaxy cluster mergers, X-shaped radio galaxies, dwarf galaxies, and more!

I am a fifth year Ph.D. candidate at George Mason University and the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory. My research is spread across a variety of topics and systems, all connected under the umbrellas of radio astrophysics and the growth and evolution of galaxies and galaxy clusters. I am very passionate about radio astrophysics and exploring our universe through this underused lens. While my thesis will focus on dual AGN and galaxy cluster mergers, by research interests extend into the realms of radio galaxies, dwarf galaxies, intermediate-mass black holes, X-shaped radio galaxies, and much more!
