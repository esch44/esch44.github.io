My Research

My thesis research is primarily focused on radio astrophysics, and investigates a variety of systems and processes. My research interests include systems with multiple active galactic nuclei, the formation and evolution of supermassive black holes, and the existence of intermediate mass black holes in dwarf galaxies. On a larger scale, I am also interested in the evolution of galaxy clusters and their mergers, including how these systems can be used to investigate dark matter, amongst other fundamental characteristics of our universe.

Dual AGNs

These systems are vital to our understanding of the formation and evolution of supermassive black holes, and yet less than fifty have been confirmed.

Galaxy Cluster Mergers

These are amongst the largest gravitationally-bound systems in our universe, and can be used to investigate physics on cluster scales, as well as dark matter.

Dwarf Galaxies

These systems are thought to harbor the elusive intermediate mass black holes.
