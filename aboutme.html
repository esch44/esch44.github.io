About Me

Hello! I am a Ph.D. candidate at George Mason University, where I work in the Black Hole-Galaxy Connection Group with Dr. Shobita Satyapal. I am a fifth year graduate student, and expect to defend my thesis and graduate in the spring of 2025. I primarily work at the U.S. Naval Research Laboratory, where my thesis advisor is Dr. Tracy Clarke. My research focuses on galaxy clusters and AGN, and is connected under the umbrella of radio astrophysics and the growth and evolution of galaxies and galaxy clusters.

Outside of Research

Outside the realm of research, I am also an active participant in outreach efforts, including as Vice President of the Spectrum student group at GMU, which focuses on diversity, equity, and inclusion in the sciences. I have a lot of experience as a teaching assistant, tutor, and peer mentor that I hope to use to benefit the next generation of unerrepresented astrophysicists.

I can also be found practicing the piano, spending quality time with Spike, my pet hedgehog, and competitively latin dancing!
