Dwarf Galaxies with Candidate IMBHs

Dwarf galaxies are critical to understanding the formation and evolution of supermassive black holes (SMBHs) and their host galaxies. There remains considerable uncertainty in the current evolution models, and this is partly due to the lack of observationa evidence for black holes with masses between 100 and 10,000 solar masses, knwon as intermediate mass black holes (IMBHs).

Coronal Lines

Low-metallicity dwarf galaxies are promising potential hosts of these IMBHs. They are additionally important as they may represent local, observable analogs of primordial galaxies that are difficult to observe due to their high redshifts. Numerous methods have been proposed in the search for IBMHs. However, separating emission driven by extreme star formation and emission driven by these low-mass IMBHs is not trivial. Full characterization of the host galaxies is necessary, as are multiwavelength observations. A new promising diagnostic for detecting accreting IMBHs in dwarf galaxies is coronal lines, the highly ionized fine structure emission lines. These can identify IMBH candidates with no other evidence of accretion.

My work focuses on two of these low-mass, low-metallicity dwarf galaxy targets, both of which have confirmed coronal line detections that may indicate an accreting IMBH. We proposed to follow these unique targets with ALMA observations that will characterize the dust emission and confirm the accretion activity, in addition to constraining any AGN contribution to the dust emission. Additionally, follow-up JWST observations are underway!

Science Question

What are the properties of the cold dust components of these dwarf galaxies? Can we confirm the presence of an AGN by fitting the SED with models that include and AGN (such as CIGALE)? What are the star formation conditions in these targets? Do we detect the various CO lines that are good tracers of the molecular star-forming resevoir? Can we place limits in the case of a non-detection?
